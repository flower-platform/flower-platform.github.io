Images​

Source of the image. Reproductibility. I.e. I want to take the screenshot again:​

Where (what screen) do I need to go? What settings do I need to apply in that screen (e.g. a filter to select only a few records)? What other steps in othe screens?

Manual case: the difficulty may range from: [easy/straight forward, hard/e.g. using a special client DB, etc].

Semi-auto case: ideally the screenshot is taken from a step of the test. In such cases, all is good. It's semi-auto and not auto, because a human touch is needed to crop. And mayb add a bit of annotations (e.g. a red rectangle, an arrow). Callout w/ text is discouraged; it needs to be in the text, not image. It's easier to modify.

Thoughts about an auto case. In this case the dev would need to specify the image/step/focus. This is doable. But a manual info is needed: clip rectangle. Ideally relative to the focused element. Not x/y relative to the image. And to be safe it should be the cropped photo by default, w/ a small button to open also the big photo. Because if the crop fails (slightly or miserably), at least there is an escape hatch.

How to crop?​

Too big. Screenshots of the full screen take too much space. Often they are shrinked: so we don't see well the detail/text. And having many full screen pages gives the sensation of all being the same, and the attention span decreases.

Too small. It does contain the information that we want to transfer. But context is missing. A new user might want to know what screen is that. And even for a pro user: there may be several screens w/ this table.

Maybe right size.

  • crop top:
    • I eliminated the blue area. Maybe in the future will change. And it takes a lot of space w/ no info. However I let a few pixels of blue. Because the picture is rendered on a white background, having a bit of border informs the viewer where the picture actually begins. It's a bit better.
    • I let the breadcrumbs. So the viewer sees the screen. And even us. If we want to take the screenshot again => we know where to go
  • crop right: I cropped so that we can see the current tab.
  • crop bottom: I eliminated info that's not needed (no of records). And maybe in the future the tab will have other records, etc.

Focus on things​

By default, no mouse cursor in the image. Sometimes we forget it during screenshot. We need to pay attention.

Only one thing to focus: I'd recommend to put the mouse pointer there and take the screenshot. It's easier for cases such as:

Several things? Then red rectangle, arrow or similar.

Image name​

Among the scenarios w/ images, most of them have a single image. We recommend:

@img image.png

This minimizes the clicks/operations during the phase of authoring the Featurebook. In VS code, click on Go to dir featurebook-img/.... Paste the image there. The image will be named image.png.
